The above Rebalance Analysis page shows only single-period trade analysis. However, Portfolio Managers are often more interested about delta trade analysis.
Its more insightful to know delta(changes) in portfolio composition versus the pre-rebalance situation. It important to know why and how the portfolio composition is changing and the heuristics of trade attributions.

Following visuals will be helpful for Portfolio managers (PMs) to track the exposure changes across multiple dimensions, such as:

  • Exposure changes by sectors or industries
  • Exposure changes by regions or countries
  • Changes in the compositions (or tilts) in risk factors exposures
  • Changes in the compositions of alpha factor exposures

Trade Attribution Analysis

PM may also want to attribute reasons behind the trades, which may include:

  • Alpha-seeking trades: For instance, a trade might be executed to increase the portfolio's exposure to high-alpha securities. This can be validated by plotting Alpha Scores Vs. Trade Direction. Ideally, the buy-side trades should be concentrated in securities with higher alpha scores, while sell-side trades should involve lower-alpha securities.
  • Risk reduction (Benchmark alignment) trades: In the case of benchmark-constrained investors, some trades may need to be executed to manage active (or under) weights even if the alpha score of the security is not favorable.

This trade attribution analytics can be combined or validated against the Portfolio Optimizer solution logs (if the optimizer provides insights into details of the optimization process).


Tracking Error Decomposition Analytics

Though it is less relevant for Long-Short market neutral portfolios, for benchmark constrained investors tracking error decompositions are an important and closely monitored analytics for compliance reasons.

Tracking error decompositions can be done by:
  • by industry/sector/country/region
  • by risk factors

For large AUM investors, other trade execution analytics related to implementation shortfall, slippage, and market impact costs could be relevant and interesting.
